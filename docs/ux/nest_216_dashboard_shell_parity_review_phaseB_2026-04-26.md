## Result

This pass improved the app again. `Assistant` now feels much more native to the
Nest product language, and the dashboard is less duplicated and less mechanical
than before. The app is still not yet fully at canonical founder parity.

## What improved in Phase B

- The duplicate date/weather treatment inside the dashboard hero was removed.
- The dashboard hero is visually calmer and less over-explained.
- The support rail cards have softer supporting copy and slightly more intimate
  hierarchy.
- The assistant surface now stages guidance, quote, composer, and support cards
  more like a real room than a raw tool panel.

## Remaining Gaps

## 1. Dashboard hero still needs proportion correction

- The hero is improved, but still reads taller and airier than the founder
  target.
- The metrics still feel too low and too detached from the narrative block.
- The progress ring and title block are still not as tightly composed as in the
  reference.

## 2. Header and hero still do not fully melt into one editorial entry

- The transition between page heading and main hero is better, but still a bit
  too segmented.
- The founder target still feels more continuous and less like stacked panels.

## 3. Sidebar is stronger but not yet luxurious enough

- The rail branding and footer are better than before, but still slightly too
  product-utility in typographic rhythm.
- The identity zones can still become calmer and more precious.

## 4. Support rail still needs closer material parity

- `Journal`, `Quick add`, and `Life areas` are now in the right family, but
  their surfaces still feel a touch too card-like compared to the target.

## 5. Assistant room needs one more conversational refinement

- The room is now valid and coherent.
- It still needs:
  - stronger answer-stage styling once responses appear,
  - slightly richer prompt choreography,
  - more premium spacing balance between left conversation stage and right
    support column.

Continue the loop with one more targeted parity pass focused mostly on:
