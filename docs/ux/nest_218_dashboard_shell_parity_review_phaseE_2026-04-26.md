## Result

This pass materially improves the app. The workspace shell now reads much more
like one calm product object, and the dashboard support rail sits closer to the
founder composition. The app is still not yet at full canonical parity.

## What improved in Phase E

- The dashboard now uses one desktop shell grid instead of carrying an older
  hero-wrapper composition.
- The top entry is lighter and more editorial because the page title no longer
  sits inside a separate heavy card.
- The sidebar account zone is calmer and closer to the founder reference.
- The support rail behaves more like one integrated right column.

## Remaining Gaps

## 1. The sidebar still feels a bit too wide and product-heavy

- It is much closer than before, but the rail still has more navigation weight
  and footer utility than the founder target.
- The brand block and account/footer stack still need one more luxury pass in
  spacing and quietness.

## 2. The hero still needs a final typography-and-density pass

- The hero composition is now valid, but the title block still feels bolder and
  denser than the reference.
- The metric row still reads slightly more mechanical than editorial.

## 3. The focus and timeline pair still need finer proportion tuning

- The overall relationship is close, but the focus card still dominates more
  heavily than in the reference.
- The timeline panel needs subtler separators and slightly closer spatial
  rhythm to the founder image.

## 4. The support rail cards still need softer material treatment

- `Journal`, `Quick add`, and `Life areas` are structurally right.
- Their surfaces still feel a little more card-based and less painterly than
  the canonical target.

## 5. The assistant room is stronger, but not yet a canonical peer of the dashboard

- It belongs to the same family now.
- It still needs one more response-state and premium-spacing pass to feel fully
  equal to the dashboard surface quality.
