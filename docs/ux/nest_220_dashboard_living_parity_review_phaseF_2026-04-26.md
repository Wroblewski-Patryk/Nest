## Result

This pass meaningfully improves the product feel. The dashboard now carries
more quiet life and less utility harshness, and the assistant room is more
clearly part of the same Nest atmosphere. The app still does not yet fully
match the canonical founder image.

## What improved in Phase F

- The rail is calmer and less heavy than before.
- The dashboard hero has better density and less mechanical metric treatment.
- The center composition feels more balanced because the focus card and day
  flow are closer in weight.
- The right-rail cards are softer and more painterly.
- The UI now has gentle liveness through restrained motion and hover-material
  response.
- The assistant answer state is better prepared for premium response rendering.

## Remaining Gaps

## 1. The dashboard hero still needs a final visual-composition pass

- The hero is much better, but the title still feels a little too bold and
  blocky compared with the founder target.
- The metric line is calmer now, but still reads slightly more system-like than
  editorial.

## 2. The left rail still needs one more serenity reduction

- It is slimmer and calmer, but it still claims a touch more attention than the
  founder reference.
- Navigation labels, footer quote, and account area can still become quieter.

## 3. The focus card remains slightly more dominant than the canonical target

- It is better proportioned now, but still carries more visual authority than
  in the reference image.
- The founder target feels a little airier and less contrast-heavy in that
  block.

## 4. The assistant idle state still needs more canonical warmth

- The room belongs to the family now.
- Without a response present, the left stage still feels too sparse compared to
  the richness of the dashboard landing surface.

## 5. The final system pass should shift from “screen polish” to “canonical finish”

- The next pass should focus on the last 10 to 15 percent:
  - final hero typography,
  - final rail quietness,
  - final support-card softness,
  - richer assistant idle and response choreography.
